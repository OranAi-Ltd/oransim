## Generation Process

The synthetic data is generated by `backend/scripts/gen_synthetic_data.py` (landing in v0.2). The generator:

1. **Demographic sampling** — IPF-calibrated age × gender × region × income × platform-usage distribution (priors from public Chinese statistical yearbooks)
2. **Creative sampling** — text templates stratified by vertical (beauty / fashion / 3C / F&B)
3. **KOL sampling** — fan-count × niche × engagement rate distribution from public aggregate statistics
4. **Outcome simulation** — Hill saturation + frequency fatigue + Hawkes diffusion, calibrated to match aggregate published metrics

**Crucially**: no real KOL, note, user, or campaign data enters the synthetic set. All training samples are drawn from distributions, not from real instances.

## Intended Use

- Training the shipped LightGBM quantile world model
- Benchmarking (see [OrancBench](../../docs/en/benchmarks/README.md))
- Testing platform adapters
- Research reproducibility

## Known Limitations

1. **Distribution fidelity** — synthetic data matches published aggregate statistics, not real individual campaigns. Performance on real data may differ.
2. **Chinese-market bias** — XHS-focused priors. Global-market generalization requires additional calibration.
3. **No multimodal** — text + tabular only in v0.1-v0.5; image/video creative generation on roadmap.
4. **No adversarial examples** — fraud, bot, spam patterns not explicitly modeled.

## Ethical Considerations

- Synthetic data **does not** contain personal information about real users, KOLs, or brands
- OSS users training on this dataset can freely redistribute derived models under Apache-2.0
- Real-data training pipelines (used for the paid Enterprise Edition) are separate from this dataset and not shipped in OSS
